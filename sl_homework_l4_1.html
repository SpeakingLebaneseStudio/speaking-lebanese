Homework Brief

Order like you mean it

Everything from this lesson — vocabulary, demonstratives, requests — comes together in one short recording.

You've learned how to point at what you want, ask for it, and describe it. This assignment puts all of it in your mouth at once — imagine you're sitting down at a Lebanese restaurant and record yourself ordering out loud.

The assignment

Record a 45-second voice memo ordering a meal at a Lebanese restaurant. Say what you want to eat and drink, point at something using hayda or haydi, and ask for the bill at the end.

Use this lesson's building blocks
  • baddi... — to say what you want
  • 36eeni... — to ask for something directly
  • hayda / haydi — to point at something on the table
  • el 7séb, law sama7t — to ask for the bill

Don't worry about sounding perfect — the goal is fluency of thought, not a rehearsed script. If you stumble, keep going. Real conversations don't have a rewind button either.
