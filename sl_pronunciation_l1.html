A1 Beginner Survival — Lesson 1 — Activity 4

Pronunciation Training

Three sounds. Every lesson. These are the sounds English speakers find hardest — and the ones that appear most in Lebanese. Master them here, and every word becomes easier.

7

The 7 sound

ح

Constrict the back of your throat as if fogging a mirror — then push air through. Heavy, breathy H. Much heavier than the English H in "hello."

ArabiziArabicEnglishListen
mar7abaمرحباhello
saba7 el khayrصباح الخيرgood morning
keef 7aalakكيف حالكhow are you
Shadow Prompt — Sound 7
mar7aba, keef 7aalak?

Listen. Repeat during the silence. Listen again and compare. You will hear the phrase twice with a 3-second gap.

The 7 sound feels unfamiliar at first. Most learners get it within a week. Yours just needs practice.

3

The 3 sound

ع

Squeeze the very back of your throat — the place you feel when you swallow. Hold briefly, release with a vowel. No English equivalent.

ArabiziArabicEnglishListen
3afwanعفواًexcuse me / you're welcome
shou elwad3شو الوضعwhat's up
min 3youniمن عيونيmy pleasure (lit. from my eyes)
Shadow Prompt — Sound 3
3afwan, shou elwad3?

Listen. Repeat during the silence. Listen again and compare. You will hear the phrase twice with a 3-second gap.

The 3 sound has no equivalent in English. That is normal. Your ear will adjust before your mouth does — that is the right order.

2

The 2 sound

ء

Stop all airflow completely at the back of your throat for a split second — like the catch between syllables in "uh-oh." Then release.

ArabiziArabicEnglishListen
2ahlanأهلاًwelcome
2ismإسمname
halla2هلأnow
Shadow Prompt — Sound 2
2ahlan, shou 2ismak?

Listen. Repeat during the silence. Listen again and compare. You will hear the phrase twice with a 3-second gap.

The glottal stop already exists in English — you use it every time you say "uh-oh." You are further along than you think.
