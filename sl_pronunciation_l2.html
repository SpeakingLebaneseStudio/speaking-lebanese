A1 Beginner Survival — Lesson 2 — Activity 3

Pronunciation Training

Two new sounds this lesson. Both appear constantly in numbers and job titles — master them here, and the rest of the lesson gets easier.

5

The 5 sound

خ

A raspy, scraping sound made at the very back of the throat — like gently clearing your throat, or gargling without water. Similar to the "ch" in the Scottish "loch" or German "Bach." No true English equivalent.

Shadow Prompt — Sound 5
Bi heyda el hotel, fee 5amseen 2oudat mech 5amsta3ch.

In this hotel there are fifty rooms, not fifteen. Listen. Repeat during the silence. Listen again and compare. You will hear the phrase twice with a 3-second gap.

The 5 sound lives at the back of the throat, not the front of the mouth — that's the whole trick. A few tries and it stops feeling strange.

6

The 6 sound

ط

A heavier, rounder T. Flatten the back of the tongue, press it firmly against the roof of the mouth, and tighten the throat slightly as you release it — thicker and more resonant than an English T.

Shadow Prompt — Sound 6
El 6obbaa5 bya3mol 2akl 6ayyeb.

The cook makes good food. Listen. Repeat during the silence. Listen again and compare. You will hear the phrase twice with a 3-second gap.

The 6 sound is just a heavier T — you already make every part of this sound in English. You're only turning up the weight.
